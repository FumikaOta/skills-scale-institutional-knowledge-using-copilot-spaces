# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Clarify requirements, estimate work, provide technical feedback
- **Project Managers**: Update on progress, flag blockers and dependencies
- **UX Designers**: Implement designs, discuss technical constraints and feasibility
- **Data Analysts**: Implement instrumentation, troubleshoot data collection issues
- **Release Manager**: Coordinate deployments, provide release artifacts and documentation

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Define requirements, review implementations, prioritize technical debt
- **Project Managers**: Align on timelines and scope, manage stakeholder expectations
- **UX Designers**: Collaborate on user research, validate design solutions
- **Data Analysts**: Review metrics and insights, define success criteria
- **Customer Support Liaison**: Gather customer feedback, prioritize pain points

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Track progress, manage dependencies, remove blockers
- **Product Managers**: Align on scope and priorities, manage trade-offs
- **Release Manager**: Coordinate release timing with project milestones
- **Data Analysts**: Monitor project health metrics and velocity
- **Customer Support Liaison**: Incorporate customer feedback into planning

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers create intuitive, user-centered experiences by researching user needs, designing interfaces, and validating designs through testing. They collaborate closely with product managers and developers to ensure features are both functional and delightful.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Define user flows and interaction patterns
- Maintain design systems and component libraries
- Collaborate with developers on implementation details
- Validate designs through A/B testing and user feedback

### Goals
- Improve user satisfaction and engagement
- Reduce friction in user workflows
- Ensure accessibility and inclusive design
- Maintain design consistency across products

### Typical Communication
- Weekly design reviews with product and engineering
- User research findings and testing reports
- Design specifications and handoff documentation
- Collaboration in design tools (Figma, Sketch, etc.)

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Align on user needs, prioritize design work, validate solutions
- **Developers**: Hand off designs, clarify implementation details, review built features
- **Data Analysts**: Use analytics to inform design decisions and measure impact
- **Customer Support Liaison**: Gather user feedback and pain points to address

---

## Data Analyst

### Role Summary
Data Analysts transform raw data into actionable insights that drive product and business decisions. They design metrics, build dashboards, and conduct analyses to measure success and identify opportunities.

### Responsibilities
- Define and track key performance indicators (KPIs)
- Build and maintain dashboards and reporting tools
- Conduct data analysis to support decision-making
- Validate hypotheses through A/B testing and experimentation
- Identify trends, patterns, and anomalies in data
- Ensure data quality and integrity

### Goals
- Enable data-driven decision making
- Provide timely, accurate insights to stakeholders
- Identify opportunities for optimization and growth
- Maintain reliable data infrastructure and reporting

### Typical Communication
- Weekly metrics reviews with product and engineering
- Analysis reports and dashboards
- Experimentation results and recommendations
- Data documentation and definitions

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Define success metrics, validate product hypotheses, inform roadmap
- **Developers**: Collaborate on instrumentation and data collection
- **UX Designers**: Provide usage data and user behavior insights
- **Project Managers**: Track project health metrics and delivery performance

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers orchestrate the software release process from development through production deployment. They coordinate across teams, manage release schedules, and ensure smooth, reliable deployments with minimal risk.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and coordinate release schedules and windows
- Manage release branches and version control strategy
- Oversee release testing and validation procedures
- Coordinate with stakeholders on deployment timing
- Maintain release documentation and runbooks
- Track release metrics and post-deployment health
- Manage rollback procedures when needed

### Goals
- Ensure predictable, low-risk releases
- Minimize deployment-related incidents
- Improve release frequency and reliability
- Maintain clear communication around releases

### Typical Communication
- Release planning meetings with engineering and product
- Release notes and deployment announcements
- Go/no-go meetings before major releases
- Post-deployment status updates

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Coordinate code freeze, branching, and deployment timing
- **Project Managers**: Align release schedules with project milestones
- **Customer Support Liaison**: Communicate release changes and prepare support team
- **Product Managers**: Prioritize features for releases, manage release scope

---

## Customer Support Liaison

### Role Summary
Customer Support Liaisons bridge the gap between customers and the product team. They gather feedback, escalate issues, and ensure customer needs inform product development while keeping support teams informed of changes.

### Responsibilities
- Collect and synthesize customer feedback and pain points
- Escalate critical customer issues to engineering
- Provide product team with support ticket trends and insights
- Prepare support team for new features and changes
- Document known issues and workarounds
- Advocate for customer needs in product discussions

### Goals
- Improve customer satisfaction and retention
- Reduce support burden through product improvements
- Ensure timely resolution of customer issues
- Enable support team with clear documentation and training

### Typical Communication
- Weekly sync with product and engineering on top issues
- Customer feedback reports and trend analysis
- Support readiness briefings before releases
- Issue escalation and status updates

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Share customer insights, validate feature priorities
- **Developers**: Escalate bugs and technical issues, clarify behavior
- **Release Manager**: Stay informed of upcoming releases, prepare support documentation
- **UX Designer**: Provide user feedback and usability concerns from support tickets
